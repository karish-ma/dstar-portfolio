Digital STARs

A National Digital Literacy Programme

A competency-based blended learning programme for Romanian citizens, delivered through a national network of public libraries. Built on DigCompRo - Romania's national digital competence standard - this curriculum maps each learning unit to measurable outcomes across three life contexts: private, professional, and civic.

LU 1 · Area 0 - Devices and Foundations
Private Professional

Basics of Digital Devices and Internet

"What can I do with a digital device, and how do I get it set up for my needs?"

⏱ 1 hour 📷 Mobile + Desktop
Area 0 · 0.1Operating Digital Devices Area 0 · 0.2Accessing and Engaging Digitally

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the main functions of common digital devices (computer, tablet, smartphone)
  • Install apps and software, and update them when needed
  • Change settings to personalise and optimise device use
  • Know different storage options - local, USB, and cloud - and when to use each
  • Use online tools and video tutorials to develop digital skills independently

Key Messages

Digital devices are tools - just like a telephone or a car. You don't need to understand how they work on the inside to use them well. Start with what you need, and build from there.
Your settings control your experience. Taking 10 minutes to configure a new device for your needs makes everything easier from day one.

Instructional Strategy

Short explainer video Facilitator-led device demo Device-to-use-case matching Group discussion

Assessment

Formative Self-reflection questions on device use; hands-on task: change 3 settings on their own device.
LU 2 · Area 4 - Safety and Security
Private Professional

Security and Safety Settings

"How do I protect my personal information online - and how do I spot when something isn't safe?"

⏱ 1 hour 🎪 Scenario-Based
Area 4 · 4.1Protecting Your Devices Area 4 · 4.2Protecting Personal Data and Privacy

Learning Objectives

  • Recognise phishing emails and suspicious links before clicking
  • Create and manage strong passwords; understand two-factor authentication
  • Adjust privacy settings on devices, apps, and social media accounts
  • Know which personal data should never be shared online
  • Understand why keeping operating systems and antivirus software updated matters

Key Messages

A strong password is your front door. Using the same password everywhere is like using the same key for your home, your car, and your bank. A free password manager solves this.
Pause before you click. Phishing messages rely on urgency - "your account will be closed", "you've won a prize". If it creates panic, slow down and verify.

Instructional Strategy

Phishing simulation activity Spot-the-red-flag exercise Personal security checklist Group debrief on real examples

Assessment

Formative Identify 3 red flags in a sample phishing email; complete a personal safety checklist.
LU 3 · Area 2 - Communication and Collaboration
Private Professional

Communications and Operating Online

"What digital tools can I use to communicate - and how do I choose the right one for each situation?"

⏱ 1 hour 💬 Hands-On Practice
Area 2 · 2.1Interacting via Digital Technologies Area 2 · 2.2Sharing Through Digital Tools

Core Objectives - Private Track

  • Send, reply to, and forward emails with correct formatting and attachments
  • Use messaging apps and video calls for personal communication
  • Navigate social media platforms and manage privacy controls
  • Know how to behave online according to context (formal vs. informal)

Additional Objectives - Professional Track

  • Use advanced video-conferencing features (moderating, recording, screen-sharing)
  • Use cloud services (Google Drive, OneDrive) to share and collaborate on files
  • Understand digital communication etiquette in professional contexts

Key Message

Email is formal and leaves a record. Messaging is immediate and informal. Video calls replace in-person presence. Choosing the right channel is a professional skill.

Assessment

Formative Compose a well-formatted email; match 5 communication scenarios to the most appropriate digital tool.
LU 4 · Area 1 - Information and Data Literacy
Private Professional

Seeking Information and Researching Online

"How do I find what I need online - and how do I know whether I can trust it?"

⏱ 1 hour 🔍 Inquiry-Based
Area 1 · 1.1Browsing, Searching and Filtering Area 1 · 1.2Evaluating What You Find Online Area 1 · 1.3Managing and Organising Digital Content

Learning Objectives

  • Use specific keywords to find information quickly on search engines and within files
  • Use advanced search features to filter and refine results
  • Differentiate promoted (paid) content from organic search results
  • Evaluate a source's purpose and reliability before trusting it
  • Recognise misinformation patterns including fake news and manipulated images
  • Organise digital content using folders and consistent file naming

Professional Track - Additional Scope

  • Use job boards and professional platforms (LinkedIn, eJobs Romania) to search for opportunities
  • Evaluate the reliability of online job listings and identify recruitment scams

Key Message

Search engines surface popularity, not truth. The top result isn't always the most accurate. Cross-reference sources before acting on what you find online.

Assessment

Formative Evaluate 3 given websites for credibility using a provided checklist; identify one example of misleading content and explain why.
LU 5 · Area 3 - Digital Content Creation
Private · 1 hr Professional · 2 hrs

Content Creation and Editing

"How do I create, edit, and share digital content - documents, images, audio, and video?"

⏱ 1-2 hours 📄 Hands-On Project
Area 3 · 3.1Developing Digital Content Area 3 · 3.2Re-elaborating and Remixing Content

Core Objectives - Both Tracks

  • Create and edit digital text documents (Word, Google Docs, OpenDocument formats)
  • Work with images: crop, resize, and use basic editing tools
  • Understand copyright and Creative Commons licensing when reusing content
  • Create something new by mixing different types of content (text, images, audio)

Extended Objectives - Professional Track Only

  • Produce multimedia presentations with text, images, audio, and video
  • Apply formatting standards for professional documents (CVs, reports)
  • Collaborate on shared documents with tracked changes

Key Messages

Creating digital content is a communication skill, not just a technical one. The right format for the right audience makes your message land.
Just because something is online doesn't mean it's free to use. Always check licensing. Creative Commons makes it easy to find content you can legally reuse.

Assessment

Formative Private: Create a formatted document using a template; identify one licensed image they could legally reuse.

Summative Professional: Produce a 3-5 slide multimedia presentation rated against a content and design rubric.
LU 6 · Areas 2 and 5 - Citizen Life
Mandatory for All

Citizen Life and eGovernment

"What government and civic services are available online, and how do I participate responsibly as a digital citizen?"

⏱ 2 hours 🏛 Applied + Exploratory
Area 2 · 2.3Civic Participation and Digital Rights Area 2 · 2.4Managing Your Digital Identity Area 5 · 5.4Digital Entrepreneurship

Learning Objectives

  • Navigate Romanian eGovernment portals and access public services (health, tax, ID)
  • Complete and submit online government forms accurately and securely
  • Know your digital rights, including data privacy protections under Romanian and EU law
  • Understand what a digital identity is and how to manage it across platforms
  • Recognise online messages that target certain groups or spread harmful content
  • Engage responsibly in online civic spaces (forums, news comments, petitions)
  • Explore opportunities to participate in the digital economy and online business

Romania-Specific Context

🇷🇴 gov.ro portal navigation eID and digital authentication Online health appointment booking ANAF tax portal

Key Messages

Digital government services save time and reduce barriers. Knowing how to find and use official Romanian portals is increasingly a civic right and responsibility.
Your digital identity is everything that identifies you online. In the EU, you have the right to protect it - and the right to be forgotten.

Assessment

Summative Complete a simulated eGovernment form; identify one public service relevant to their own situation; reflect on one digital right that applies to them personally.

04 - What the Numbers Mean

DigCompRo Framework

Romania's national Digital Competence standard, adapted from EU DigComp. Numbers are reference codes - like chapter and section numbers in a book.

How to read the area codes: Each unit is tagged with a DigCompRo reference like Area 1 · 1.2. "Area 1" means the skill belongs to Information and Data Literacy. "1.2" specifically means evaluating what you find - not just finding it (that's 1.1). The decimal is a sub-section reference within that area.
Area 0
Devices and Foundations
The basics: getting online, using devices, having the right attitude toward learning new tools.
0.1Operating digital devices and technologies
0.2Engaging with the digital world continuously, ethically, and inclusively
Area 1
Information and Data Literacy
Finding information online, judging whether it's reliable, and keeping it organised.
1.1Browsing, searching, filtering - finding what you need quickly
1.2Evaluating - judging whether what you've found is accurate and trustworthy
1.3Managing - organising and storing digital information so you can find it again
Area 2
Communication and Collaboration
How you interact with others digitally - from email and messaging to civic participation and your online identity.
2.1Interacting - using email, messaging, video calls effectively
2.2Sharing - posting, forwarding, and collaborating responsibly
2.3Civic engagement - participating in online public life; rights and responsibilities
2.4Digital identity - managing how you present yourself across platforms
Area 3
Digital Content Creation
Making things digitally - documents, images, videos, presentations - and knowing the rules around reusing others' work.
3.1Creating - making documents, images, audio, video, and presentations
3.2Re-elaborating - legally remixing and adapting existing digital content
Area 4
Safety and Security
Protecting yourself and your data online - from passwords and phishing to privacy settings and software updates.
4.1Protecting devices - antivirus, updates, firewalls, secure access
4.2Protecting personal data - privacy settings, passwords, what not to share
Area 5
Problem Solving and Entrepreneurship
Using digital tools to solve problems - including accessing public services, troubleshooting, and finding digital business opportunities.
5.2Identifying digital needs - finding the right tool or platform for a goal
5.4Digital entrepreneurship - spotting and acting on digital economy opportunities

05 - Assessment Design

Four-Stage Assessment

Assessment is woven through the entire learning journey - from diagnostic entry to certification at the close.

Stage 1
📋
Pre-Assessment
DSI · 30 min
EU Digital Skills Indicator questions establish baseline and route learner to Private or Professional track. Results personalise - never exclude.
Stage 2
💡
Unit Self-Reflection
Embedded throughout
Short self-reflection questions after each unit. Learners already at "basic skills" level can skip and mark complete - or review for reinforcement.
Stage 3
📊
Post-Test
DSI + Unit Items · 30 min
Same DSI items as Stage 1, plus course-specific questions. Measures learning gain. Results feed back into individual progress reporting.
Stage 4
🏆
Certification
Randomised Bank · Retakeable
Randomly drawn items from validated DigCompRo item bank. Passing score confirms at least basic digital skills. Certificate issued via Moodle LMS.

06 - Design Rationale

Curriculum Methodology

Digital STARs - Bridging Romania's digital divide.

Digital STARs is grounded in DigCompRo - Romania's national Digital Competence framework, aligned to EU DigComp. The six competency areas (Devices, Information, Communication, Content, Safety, and Problem Solving) set the outcomes that every learner works toward, and the EU Digital Skills Indicator provides the pre- and post-assessment measure of whether they got there.

But competency frameworks define what to teach - not why it matters to any specific learner. The curriculum uses life events as the instructional context: a young person drafting their first CV is practising Area 3 (Content Creation); an adult using e-banking or accessing government e-services - paying fines online or authenticating via RoEID - is applying Area 5 (Problem Solving). The three tracks (Private, Professional, Citizen) route each of the programme's 100,000 participants to the life contexts most relevant to them, while the shared Citizen track ensures common civic digital literacy across every cohort.

The target population includes socio-economically disadvantaged individuals, people with disabilities, unskilled workers, retired adults, women, and young people not currently in education or employment. Many also carry digital skepticism - an active distrust of technology, not just unfamiliarity with it. The curriculum addresses this directly: debunking common myths, demonstrating concrete personal benefits, and building attitudes and confidence alongside knowledge and skills.

←
Backward Design
DigCompRo outcomes defined first. DSI assessment items second. Learning activities designed last - every choice justified by what learners need to demonstrate.
🌟
Life Events as Context
Private, Professional, and Citizen tracks use real-life situations - booking a health appointment, applying for a job, paying tax online - so skills transfer immediately.
📚
Blended Delivery
10 hrs self-paced eLearning on Moodle (SCORM 1.2), combined with librarian-facilitated support at 700+ public libraries nationwide.
☑
Accessible by Design
EN 301 549 compliant. Mobile-first for Private track. Optimised for low bandwidth. Short lessons capped at 30 minutes each.
🛠
Modular Architecture
Six standalone units that can be updated independently as Romanian digital services evolve - without redesigning the whole programme.
